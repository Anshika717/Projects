DR. APJ ABDUL KALAM

The Missile Man of INDIA
Time line of Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam
  • Birth: Born on 15th October 1931 in a muslim family of Rameshwaram, Tamil nadu.
  • Family: His father's name was Jainulabdeen Marakayar, mother's name was Ashiamma and 4 siblings as 3 elder brothers and a younger sister.
  • Poverty Striken Childhood: The Marakayar were wealthy people but because of the construction of The Pamban Bridge their business failed and as a result Dr. Kalam at a young age used to sell newspapers.
  • Schooling:Dr. Kalam did his schooling from Schwartz Higher Secondary school in Ramanathapuram. His teachers described him as a bright and ethusiatic learner.
  • College and Higher Studies:He graduated from physics from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli and then studied aerospace engineering from Madras Institute of Technology.
  • Early Career as a Scientist: After Graduating Dr. Kalam worked as a member of DRDO in Aeronautical Development Establishment. In his early career he was involved in the designing of a hovercraft.
  • Working in Space Organisations: Based on his work he was transferred to Indian National Committee of Space Research and worked there under VIKRAM SARABHAI.
  • Part of ISRO: Later Kalam Interviewed for ISRO and was selected by H.G.S Murthy and was made the Project Director od India's First satellite Launch Vehicle(SLV) which successfully deployed ROHINI satellite in near-earth orbit in July 1980.
  • Devotion towards space organisations: He worked as a Chief Scientific Adviser to Prime Minister and the secretary of DRDO from 1992-1999. Played a key role in Pokhran Nuclear Tests conducted in May 1998.
  • Presidency:In the year 2002 when NDA was in power it expressed its intention to elect Dr. Kalam as President of India. On 18th June 2002 Dr. Kalam filled his candidature for the President and faced of Lakshmi Sahgal. The polling was done on 15 July 2002 and counting was done on 18 July 2002. He won by securing 992,884 electoral votes and was sworn as THE 11TH PRESIDENT OF INDIA on 25th July 2002.
  • Death:Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam died on 27 July 2015 while delivering a speech on "Creating a Livable Planet Earth" in Shillong due to Cardiac Arrest.

Dr. Kalam was a pure soul who devoted his whole life for the welfare of the country.
